# Business Recommendations

This document provides business recommendations based on the insights from the E-Commerce Sales Performance Dashboard.

## 1. Strengthen Strategy for the Toys Category

The `toys` category is the main revenue driver, contributing approximately **79.56%** of total revenue.

**Recommendation:**
Maintain strong inventory planning, pricing strategy, and promotional campaigns for the toys category. Since the business depends heavily on this category, stock availability and campaign timing should be managed carefully.

## 2. Reduce Revenue Dependency on a Few Categories

The top two categories, `toys` and `garden_tools`, contribute almost **90%** of total revenue.

**Recommendation:**
Develop growth strategies for lower-performing categories through cross-selling, bundling, targeted discounts, and category-specific promotions. This can reduce revenue concentration risk and improve overall portfolio balance.

## 3. Prioritize High-Revenue States

SP, RJ, and MG are the strongest revenue-generating states, contributing more than **65%** of total revenue.

**Recommendation:**
Prioritize marketing, customer retention, and logistics support in these regions. These states can be treated as core markets because they already show strong demand and revenue contribution.

## 4. Explore Growth Potential in Lower-Contributing Regions

Several states contribute significantly less revenue compared to SP, RJ, and MG.

**Recommendation:**
Analyze customer acquisition, product demand, and delivery performance in lower-contributing regions to identify whether the issue is related to market demand, logistics coverage, product availability, or marketing exposure.

## 5. Optimize Checkout for Credit Card and Wallet Users

Credit card and wallet payments represent more than **93%** of total payment behavior.

**Recommendation:**
Prioritize user experience, reliability, and speed for credit card and wallet payment flows. Any friction in these payment methods could affect a large portion of transactions.

## 6. Monitor Late and Not-Delivered Orders

Although on-time delivery performance is strong at **91.61%**, late and not-delivered orders still represent **8.39%** of total orders.

**Recommendation:**
Analyze late and not-delivered orders by state, city, seller, and product category to identify operational bottlenecks. Delivery issues should be monitored because they may affect customer satisfaction and repeat purchase behavior.

## 7. Investigate High-Value Outlier Cities

Morrinhos generated very high revenue with only **26 orders**, resulting in an unusually high average order value.

**Recommendation:**
Investigate transaction-level data for Morrinhos to determine whether revenue was driven by high-value products, bulk purchases, premium items, or potential data anomalies.

## 8. Analyze the November 2017 Revenue Spike

Revenue reached its peak in **November 2017**, followed by a significant decline in December 2017.

**Recommendation:**
Review campaign activity, seasonal demand, product category performance, and order volume during November 2017 to understand the driver behind the revenue spike. If the increase was campaign-driven, similar strategies can be replicated in future periods.

## 9. Treat September 2018 as Incomplete Data

September 2018 shows extremely low revenue compared to previous months.

**Recommendation:**
Exclude September 2018 from trend-based conclusions or label it as a partial/incomplete month in the dashboard documentation.

## 10. Improve Average Order Value

Average Order Value helps measure the average spending per order.

**Recommendation:**
Increase AOV through bundling, product recommendations, cross-selling, upselling, and free shipping thresholds. This can increase revenue without relying only on higher order volume.
